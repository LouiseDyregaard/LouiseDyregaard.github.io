Hej! I am Louise and I search for exoplanets

I am an astronomer and I spend most of my time hunting down planets around other stars than our Sun, called exoplanets.

About Louise

I am an astronomer searching for exoplanets. I determining their mass and radius with aim of understanding their interior and composition.

I have since 2017 been a PhD student at Geneva observatory, working of radial velocity follow-up of transiting exoplanets. I am originally from Denmark where I studied physics at the Niels Bohr Institute in Copenhagen. While finishing my masters degree I worked as a student support astronomer at the Nordic Optical Telescope in La Palma, Spain. I have also worked at Gemini North with Inger Jørgensen on reducing spectra of high redshift galaxies from Hubble. In 2015/16 I worked for ESA in the Netherlands simulating transmission spectroscopy with NIRspec/JWST.

Exoplanet research

I have a wide range of research interests from transit surveys to internal structure modelling of giant planets

Transit surveys

I work with transiting exoplanets from both ground-based surveys (WASP and NGTS) as well as the space missions K2 and TESS. Planets transiting bright stars gives us unique opportunities to fully characterise the planetary systems as we can determine both the mass and radius of the planet as well as investigate the atmosphere through transmission spectroscopy.

Radial velocity

Working mainly with HARPS on the 3.6m ESO telescope and CORALIE on the Swiss 1.2m Euler telescope I follow-up planet transiting planet candidates. I am looking forward to expanding our wavelength coverage with the near-infrared high resolution spectrograph NIRPS which will join HARPS in 2021.

Transition between Neptune- and Saturn-like planets

If a giant planet turns out as a Neptune-like ice giant of a Saturn-like gas giant is believed to be determined by a stage of planet formation called runaway gas accretion. Characterising these types of planets in detail is key to our understanding of planet formation.
